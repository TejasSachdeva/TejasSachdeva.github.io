RC Car

Custom Built Remote-Controlled Racing Vehicle

Mechanical Design Electronics Control Systems 3D Printing

Project Overview

This project involved designing and building a custom remote-controlled car from scratch, focusing on performance optimization, durability, and advanced control systems for competitive racing applications. The goal was to create a high-performance RC vehicle that could compete in local racing competitions while serving as a learning platform for mechanical design and electronics integration.

Drivetrain: Gear-Based First Iteration<\/h2>

We began with a two-stage spur gear reduction (4:1 then 3:1, total 12:1) to translate the motor’s ~18,000 RPM and 0.05 N·m to roughly 1,500 RPM and ~0.6 N·m at the wheels — an effective balance for a <2 kg platform. The choice was motivated by compact packaging, high ideal efficiency (~98%), and zero slip. In practice, backlash in the 3D-printed PLA gears produced impact loading as the mesh alternated between drive and coast, increasing the Shigley dynamic factor (Kv<\/sub>) and amplifying tooth bending stress. Cyclic shock led to micro-pitting and accelerated wear; combined with PLA’s low hardness and creep at temperature, teeth degraded and ultimately stripped. If we had stayed with gears, a move to glass-fiber-reinforced nylon or sintered metal would have been necessary to meet fatigue requirements. <\/p>
